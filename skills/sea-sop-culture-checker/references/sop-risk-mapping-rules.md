# SOP/KPI Risk Mapping Rules

Use these rules after reading the target country profile. Combine clause wording, management intent, absolute score bands, and China-baseline gaps.

## Risk Level Logic

Assign Red when at least one applies:

- Clause uses strong command or punishment wording and the relevant cultural gap from China is medium or high.
- Clause creates public pressure, face-loss, individual blame, or direct escalation risk in collectivist/high-PDI contexts.
- Clause demands rigid process compliance in a low-UAI context where outcome-based flexibility is more suitable.
- Clause demands fast change or vague accountability in a higher-UAI context.
- Clause relies on long-term sacrifice language where LTO is far lower than China.

Assign Yellow when:

- Clause is directionally acceptable but needs rationale, softer wording, local manager mediation, or implementation guardrails.
- Clause has a low or medium China gap but uses unnecessarily harsh language.
- Clause is operationally necessary but communication style needs adjustment.

Assign Green when:

- Clause is clear, neutral, supportive, and has low cultural friction.
- Clause focuses on resources, collaboration, safety, customer value, or transparent process without blame language.

## Clause Type Rules

### 1. Mandatory Command

Typical wording:

- 必须
- 严禁
- 不得
- 一律
- 无条件执行
- 立即整改

Management intent:

- Ensure consistency.
- Prevent deviation.
- Reduce operational risk.

Risk mapping:

- High PDI: may generate surface compliance but reduce upward feedback if the clause bypasses local managers.
- Low or lower-than-China PDI: may be read as excessive control if not explained.
- High UAI: mandatory wording can work if rules are clear, stable, and supported.
- Low UAI: overly rigid wording may feel inefficient or bureaucratic.

Rewrite strategy:

- Keep the requirement when necessary.
- Add business rationale.
- Add support or escalation channel.
- Replace pure command tone with implementation language.

Example:

Original: 必须每日向总部汇报销售进展。

Localized Chinese: 各团队请在每日结束前提交简要销售进展小结，便于总部及时了解市场变化并提供必要支持。

Localized English: Teams should submit a brief sales progress summary at the end of each day so headquarters can understand market changes and provide timely support when needed.

### 2. Punishment And Deduction

Typical wording:

- 未完成扣罚
- 逾期处罚
- 绩效扣分
- 责任追究
- 取消奖金

Management intent:

- Strengthen accountability.
- Prevent repeated underperformance.
- Make consequences clear.

Risk mapping:

- Collectivist contexts: public or individual blame can damage trust and reduce issue reporting.
- High PDI: employees may avoid surfacing problems to protect hierarchy and face.
- Lower MAS than China: aggressive pressure may reduce motivation or create passive compliance.

Rewrite strategy:

- Separate performance correction from punishment-first language.
- Add coaching and improvement steps before formal consequences.
- Keep formal consequence only as final escalation.

Example:

Original: 未按时完成KPI的员工将扣除当月绩效奖金。

Localized Chinese: 对未按时达成KPI的情况，直属经理将先进行原因复盘并提供改进支持；如连续未改善，将按绩效管理流程处理。

Localized English: If a KPI is not achieved on time, the direct manager will first review the root causes and provide improvement support. If performance does not improve after repeated follow-up, the standard performance management process will apply.

### 3. Daily Reporting And Headquarters Visibility

Typical wording:

- 每日汇报
- 实时上报
- 每天提交
- 总部检查
- 进度必须透明

Management intent:

- Increase visibility.
- Detect risks early.
- Align headquarters and local teams.

Risk mapping:

- High PDI: direct headquarters reporting can weaken local manager authority.
- Collectivist contexts: reporting can be seen as surveillance if framed poorly.
- Low UAI: excessive reporting cadence may feel inefficient.

Rewrite strategy:

- Frame reporting as collaboration and support.
- Keep the cadence but reduce surveillance tone.
- Route through local managers when appropriate.

Example:

Original: 所有销售人员必须每日向总部汇报客户拜访情况。

Localized Chinese: 销售团队请每日提交简要客户拜访小结，由本地经理汇总关键进展和需要总部支持的问题。

Localized English: Sales teams should provide a brief daily customer visit summary. Local managers will consolidate key progress and highlight any issues where headquarters support is needed.

### 4. Public Ranking And Competition

Typical wording:

- 排名通报
- 末位淘汰
- 公开批评
- 业绩红黑榜
- 全员公示

Management intent:

- Create performance pressure.
- Increase accountability.
- Encourage competition.

Risk mapping:

- Lower MAS than China: competition pressure may be less motivating.
- Collectivist contexts: public ranking may create face-loss and team tension.
- High PDI: public criticism from senior authority can trigger silence and risk hiding.

Rewrite strategy:

- Replace public shame with private coaching and team progress review.
- Use recognition for positive examples.
- Keep performance intervention private and manager-led.

Example:

Original: 每周公布销售排名，连续两周末位者进行公开检讨。

Localized Chinese: 每周复盘销售进展，并分享优秀实践；对连续落后的情况，由直属经理进行一对一辅导并制定改进计划。

Localized English: Sales progress will be reviewed weekly, with strong practices shared across the team. For repeated underperformance, the direct manager will provide one-on-one coaching and agree on an improvement plan.

### 5. Approval Control And Local Autonomy

Typical wording:

- 需总部审批
- 不得擅自调整
- 所有事项报总部批准
- 未经批准不得执行

Management intent:

- Reduce deviation.
- Control risk.
- Keep standards consistent.

Risk mapping:

- High PDI: bypassing local managers can undermine local authority.
- Low UAI: too many approvals can feel slow and inefficient.
- Higher UAI: approval rules can be accepted if thresholds are clear.

Rewrite strategy:

- Define approval thresholds.
- Give local managers delegated authority within guardrails.
- Keep headquarters approval for high-risk exceptions.

Example:

Original: 所有价格调整必须经总部审批，未经批准不得执行。

Localized Chinese: 常规价格调整可在本地授权范围内执行；超出授权范围或涉及重大客户的调整，请提交总部审批。

Localized English: Standard price adjustments may be handled within the local delegation of authority. Adjustments beyond the approved range or involving strategic customers should be submitted to headquarters for approval.

### 6. Long-Term Sacrifice And Overtime

Typical wording:

- 为长期目标牺牲短期利益
- 必要时无条件加班
- 阶段性高强度投入
- 以公司大局为重

Management intent:

- Drive commitment.
- Support strategic goals.
- Handle peak workload.

Risk mapping:

- Much lower LTO than China: long-term sacrifice may not be persuasive without near-term value and fairness.
- Higher IVR than China: strict restraint or sacrifice framing may cause disengagement.
- Legal and labor compliance risk may also exist; flag for review.

Rewrite strategy:

- Explain business reason and expected duration.
- Add rest, compensation, or workload balancing language.
- Avoid unconditional sacrifice wording.

Example:

Original: 为达成年度目标，员工必须在必要时无条件加班。

Localized Chinese: 在业务高峰期，团队可能需要阶段性增加投入；管理者应提前沟通安排，并确保符合当地劳动法规和公司补偿政策。

Localized English: During peak business periods, teams may need to increase short-term effort. Managers should communicate schedules in advance and ensure arrangements comply with local labor regulations and company compensation policies.

## Rewrite Quality Checklist

Every localized rewrite should:

- Preserve the headquarters management intent.
- Reduce unnecessary command, punishment, surveillance, or shame language.
- Add rationale where possible.
- Add support channel or manager role where useful.
- Avoid stereotypes about local employees.
- Keep the clause operationally enforceable.
- Flag legal/HR review when the clause touches pay, working hours, discipline, termination, or employee privacy.
