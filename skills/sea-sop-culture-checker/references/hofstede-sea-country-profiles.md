# Hofstede Southeast Asia Country Profiles

## Source And Use Limits

Use these scores as a management early-warning reference for China-headquarters SOP/KPI rollout into Southeast Asia.

Source basis: user-provided table extracted from Geert Hofstede country comparison data at `https://geerthofstede.com/`. Estimated countries are marked with `(est.)` in the source table. Per the Hofstede website note, solid bar scores are treated as official/validated scores, while striped bars are estimates and are not scientifically validated.

Do not use these scores as legal advice, academic research proof, or deterministic descriptions of any individual employee.

## Dimensions

- PDI: Power Distance
- IDV: Individualism
- MAS: Masculinity
- UAI: Uncertainty Avoidance
- LTO: Long Term Orientation
- IVR: Indulgence

## Score Interpretation Rules

Use two layers of interpretation.

### Layer 1: Absolute Score Band

| Score | Band |
|---:|---|
| 0-35 | Low |
| 36-65 | Medium |
| 66-100 | High |

### Layer 2: Gap From China Baseline

Calculate `target country score - China score`.

| Absolute Gap | Gap Level | Use |
|---:|---|---|
| 0-9 | Low gap | Similar to China baseline; light localization is usually enough. |
| 10-24 | Medium gap | Meaningful difference; adjust wording, rollout framing, and manager guidance. |
| 25+ | High gap | Major difference; rewrite clauses and consider changing the implementation mechanism. |

Direction matters:

- Positive gap: target country scores higher than China.
- Negative gap: target country scores lower than China.

## Country Profiles

### Vietnam

Official scores: PDI 70, IDV 20, MAS 40, UAI 30, LTO 57, IVR 35.

Compared with China: lower PDI by 10, same IDV and UAI, lower MAS by 26, lower LTO by 30, higher IVR by 11.

Management implications:

- Strong hierarchy remains understandable, but headquarters direct control can still weaken local manager ownership.
- Lower MAS suggests aggressive ranking, competition pressure, and heroic performance language may need softer framing.
- Lower LTO than China means long-term sacrifice narratives may need more near-term support and milestones.

SOP/KPI risk signals:

- High-risk: public ranking,末位淘汰, hard punishment for missed targets, long-cycle sacrifice without near-term support.
- Safer: team progress review, coaching feedback, staged milestones, resource support, manager-mediated follow-up.

### Thailand

Official scores: PDI 64, IDV 20, MAS 34, UAI 64, LTO 32, IVR 45.

Compared with China: lower PDI by 16, same IDV, lower MAS by 32, higher UAI by 34, lower LTO by 55, higher IVR by 21.

Management implications:

- Lower MAS and much lower LTO mean intense competition, confrontation, and long-term sacrifice language may backfire.
- Higher UAI means ambiguous rules, sudden changes, or unclear escalation paths can create anxiety and avoidance.
- Face-saving and indirect communication should be considered in feedback and accountability design.

SOP/KPI risk signals:

- High-risk: sudden rule changes, public criticism, ranking pressure, vague "complete immediately" commands.
- Safer: clear rationale, predictable steps, private coaching, supportive escalation, phased rollout.

### Indonesia

Official scores: PDI 78, IDV 14, MAS 46, UAI 48, LTO 62, IVR 38.

Compared with China: similar PDI, lower IDV by 6, lower MAS by 20, higher UAI by 18, lower LTO by 25, higher IVR by 14.

Management implications:

- Hierarchy is familiar, but collectivist norms make public individual blame risky.
- Moderate higher UAI suggests detailed process explanations and stable expectations are helpful.
- Lower MAS than China suggests less aggressive competition framing.

SOP/KPI risk signals:

- High-risk: individual public blame, direct escalation over local managers, abrupt target changes.
- Safer: team-based accountability, local manager communication, clear process steps, supportive performance review.

### Malaysia

Official scores: PDI 100, IDV 26, MAS 50, UAI 36, LTO 41, IVR 57.

Compared with China: higher PDI by 20, higher IDV by 6, lower MAS by 16, higher UAI by 6, lower LTO by 46, higher IVR by 33.

Management implications:

- Very high PDI means hierarchy is salient; local leadership channels matter.
- Much lower LTO and higher IVR mean long-term sacrifice and strict restraint narratives may need practical near-term benefits.
- Direct headquarters micromanagement can bypass local authority and reduce trust.

SOP/KPI risk signals:

- High-risk: headquarters bypassing local managers, long-term sacrifice demands, strict overtime discipline without rationale.
- Safer: local leader endorsement, clear business rationale, near-term support, respectful escalation.

### Singapore

Official scores: PDI 74, IDV 20, MAS 48, UAI 8, LTO 72, IVR 46.

Compared with China: lower PDI by 6, same IDV, lower MAS by 18, lower UAI by 22, lower LTO by 15, higher IVR by 22.

Management implications:

- Similar collectivist and long-term orientation patterns make many China-style process controls understandable.
- Very low UAI means overly rigid approvals and heavy process lock-in may feel inefficient.
- Higher IVR means punitive or restraint-heavy rules may need a more balanced business rationale.

SOP/KPI risk signals:

- High-risk: excessive approvals, rigid "one-size-fits-all" procedures, punishment-first framing.
- Safer: outcome-based guardrails, clear accountability, exception channels, efficient reporting cadence.

### Philippines

Official scores: PDI 94, IDV 32, MAS 64, UAI 44, LTO 27, IVR 42.

Compared with China: higher PDI by 14, higher IDV by 12, similar MAS, higher UAI by 14, much lower LTO by 60, higher IVR by 18.

Management implications:

- High PDI supports hierarchy, but local leadership and relationship-sensitive communication are important.
- Very low LTO compared with China means distant strategic sacrifice narratives may not motivate enough by themselves.
- Higher IDV than China means overly collective responsibility language may need clearer personal role clarity.

SOP/KPI risk signals:

- High-risk: long-term sacrifice without short-term recognition, public pressure, unclear personal accountability.
- Safer: clear role expectations, near-term recognition, private feedback, manager-supported performance routines.
