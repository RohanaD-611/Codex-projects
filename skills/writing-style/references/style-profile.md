# Personal Writing Style Profile

This profile summarizes the user's observed style from four Chinese articles. Articles #1 and #2 are primary references; #3 and #4 are secondary calibration samples.

## 1. Overall Style

The user's writing is a reflective personal essay style with a clear argumentative spine. It often begins from a personal confusion, recent conversation, online observation, public meme, or external video, then expands into a broader judgment about career, AI, work, business, or social reality.

The voice is calm but not neutral. It carries dissatisfaction, skepticism, and moral concern, but usually avoids shouting. The strongest quality is "有秩序地发表自己的看法": the user does not chase hot-topic templates, but wants to think through a problem in a way that is readable, grounded, and personally honest.

## 2. Recurring Structure

Common arc:

1. Start from a small, concrete entry point.
2. Name the first emotional reaction: confusion, emptiness, shock, disappointment, unease.
3. Introduce a wider social or historical context.
4. Identify the mistaken common logic.
5. Offer a reframing.
6. Return to a practical human conclusion.

Representative structural moves:

- Personal state to concept: from "我还在找方向" to "个人定位不是想出来的".
- Historical case to career advice: from Fairchild/PayPal to "追随人才".
- Meme to social critique: from "炸薯条" to hiring-market rigidity.
- Conversation to values: from HR conversation to brand sustainability and respect for people.

## 3. Voice And Attitude

The user tends to:

- Admit uncertainty before making a judgment.
- Use personal experience as a doorway, not as pure diary.
- Critique workplace and market logic from the perspective of ordinary people.
- Care about dignity, trust, respect, and whether people are treated as humans.
- Resist both fatalism and fake optimism.
- Prefer concrete feedback and action over abstract destiny or talent narratives.

The tone often sits between:

- "我也在困惑"
- "这个逻辑可能有问题"
- "现实确实让人难受"
- "但还是需要回到具体行动"

## 4. Sentence Habits

Frequent sentence movements:

- "我以为...但..."
- "我第一反应是..."
- "但看完..."
- "我突然反应过来..."
- "真正有用的是..."
- "比起..."
- "这看起来...但..."
- "当你...时..."
- "所以..."
- "总的来说..."

The user likes contrast pairs:

- 想出来 / 做出来
- 追随公司 / 追随人才
- 理解人生 / 进入人生
- 找到你是谁 / 找到一小块价值
- 赚钱 / 取之有道
- 做生意 / 做人

## 5. Paragraphing

Paragraphs are usually short. The user often lets one sentence stand alone when it marks a turn:

- "迷茫至极，我决定找AI算命。"
- "它回得非常完整。"
- "我换不同AI又问了一遍。"
- "为什么？"

Longer paragraphs appear when explaining historical examples, social background, or business ethics. These longer paragraphs still keep a conversational explanatory flow rather than academic density.

## 6. Topic Tendencies

Strong fit:

- AI and personal positioning.
- Career planning and career anxiety.
- Hiring-market contradictions.
- Talent, organization, and professional networks.
- Work dignity and business ethics.
- Personal growth through action and feedback.
- Social observations with a mild historical frame.

Weak fit unless adapted:

- Pure entertainment.
- Hard sales copy.
- Highly formal policy analysis.
- Viral listicles.
- Generic productivity advice.

## 7. Language Preferences

Preferred wording:

- Direction and self-positioning: 方向、聚焦点、个人定位、反馈、复盘、调整、具体行动。
- Work and organization: 公司、岗位、人才、团队、上级、网络、组织、工牌、协作。
- Felt reality: 空空的、内耗、难受、迷茫、失落、震惊、焦虑、身体反应。
- Values: 尊重、信任、温度、责任、底气、商誉、连接、长期。
- Reasoning: 反直觉、真正、核心转向、换一个算法、前提、逻辑。

Avoid making the prose too polished. A small amount of everyday phrasing is part of the user's voice.

## 8. Title Style

Observed title patterns:

- A personal event plus a surprising sentence.
- "关于 X，我看过的最好答案".
- A quoted concept or meme plus "困境".
- A value proposition framed plainly.

Titles should be specific and reflective. They should not sound like content-farm titles.

## 9. Editing Principles

When improving drafts:

- Keep the personal uncertainty.
- Strengthen structure without making it rigid.
- Preserve concrete social details.
- Remove generic motivational language.
- Add transitions only when the thought jump is unclear.
- Keep criticism grounded in observed reality.
- Let the ending settle rather than explode.

## 10. English Limitation

No English source samples were provided. English writing should therefore be treated as a cross-language adaptation of the user's Chinese thinking style. It can preserve structure, tone, restraint, and argumentative movement, but not a fully verified English diction profile.
